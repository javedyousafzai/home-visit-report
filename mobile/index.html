Syrian refugee in Lebanon

Over the past three years, 2.8 million people have had to flee the civil war in Syria – nearly four in five of them women and children. The conflict has changed their lives in every way.

With so many men lost or left behind, women have had to take charge, making all the decisions, earning all the money, and providing all the care.

Syrian refugee women who head their household

More than 145,000 Syrian refugee women now run their households alone – more than a quarter of the half a million overall. For most, it has been a devastating experience.

Earlier this year, UNHCR interviewed 135 of these women in Jordan, Lebanon and Egypt, to put a human face on their experience. How do they pay their way? How do they cope with isolation and anxiety, and threats to their safety? How are their children dealing with the trauma?
